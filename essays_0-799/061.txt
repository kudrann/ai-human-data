 Dear Editor,
I am writing this letter to express my opinion on the effects of computers on society. While there are many who believe that computers have had a positive impact on our lives, I argue that they have actually harmed us in several ways.
Firstly, computers have made us more sedentary. With the rise of technology, people are spending more time sitting at their desks and less time engaging in physical activity. This has led to an increase in obesity and other health problems.
Secondly, computers have isolated us from one another. Social media platforms and online gaming communities have created a false sense of connection, but they cannot replace the human interaction that is essential for our well-being.
Finally, computers have made us more dependent on technology. We rely on GPS to navigate, smartphones to communicate, and search engines to answer questions. This has left us ill-equipped to handle real-world challenges and has diminished our problem-solving skills.
In conclusion, I believe that computers have had a negative impact on society. While they may have some benefits, these are outweighed by the harm they cause. We must take steps to limit our use of technology and reconnect with the world around us.
