 Dear Editor,

I am writing this letter to express my opinion on the impact of computers on society. As technology advances and more people use computers, it is essential to evaluate whether these advances benefit us or not. 

On one hand, computers have undoubtedly revolutionized our lives in various ways. For instance, they teach hand-eye coordination, which helps children develop their motor skills as they learn to manipulate the computer mouse. Additionally, computers enable us to access information about places and cultures we may never experience firsthand. This exposure fosters understanding and empathy among people from different backgrounds. Finally, online communication allows us to maintain relationships with friends and family members who live far away or have demanding schedules.

On the other hand, some experts express concerns about the effects of computers on society. They argue that people are spending more time sitting in front of their screens rather than engaging in physical activities, enjoying nature, or socializing with friends and family members face-to-face. This sedentary lifestyle has been linked to various health issues, including obesity and poor cardiovascular health. Moreover, excessive computer use can lead to addiction, which damages relationships and hampers personal growth.

In conclusion, computers have both positive and negative effects on people. It is essential to strike a balance between embracing technology and maintaining our physical, mental, and emotional well-being. As a society, we must encourage responsible computer use and educate ourselves about the potential risks associated with overreliance on technology.

In light of these thoughts, I urge your readers to consider the importance of striking a balance between using computers and engaging in other meaningful activities that promote our overall well-being.
